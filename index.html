How this PC bottleneck calculator works

This calculator compares relative CPU game throughput, GPU render throughput, memory headroom, memory speed, storage class, resolution, and workload. It is a planning tool, not a benchmark replacement. Game engine, settings, cooling, drivers, background apps, and motherboard power limits can change results.

  • CPU score
    Weighted toward gaming IPC, cache, boost behavior, and modern core count.
  • GPU score
    Weighted toward raster performance, VRAM class, and resolution scaling.
  • RAM score
    Checks capacity pressure and speed penalty for high-FPS and creator workloads.
  • Storage score
    Flags load-time and asset-streaming risk, especially for newer open-world games.

What "CPU bottleneck" means

A CPU bottleneck usually appears as low GPU usage, unstable frame pacing, or weak minimum FPS. It is most common at 1080p, in esports titles, simulation games, and streaming workloads.

What "GPU bottleneck" means

A GPU bottleneck means the graphics card is the main performance limiter. This is normal at 1440p, ultrawide, and 4K with high visual settings, ray tracing, or heavy texture packs.

Why RAM and storage matter

RAM and storage rarely raise average FPS like a stronger GPU can, but too little memory or a slow drive can cause stutter, long loading screens, texture pop-in, and poor multitasking.

PC bottleneck calculator FAQ

Is a bottleneck always bad?

No. Every PC has a limiting component in a given game. A "good" bottleneck is often the GPU at your target resolution because that means visual settings are using the graphics card fully.

What bottleneck percentage is acceptable?

Small gaps are normal. Treat results below about 12% as broadly balanced, 12-25% as worth checking against your games, and larger gaps as a stronger upgrade signal.

Should I upgrade CPU or GPU first?

For 1440p and 4K, GPU upgrades usually matter most. For 1080p high-refresh esports, simulation games, and streaming, CPU and RAM choices matter more than they do in GPU-bound AAA gaming.
